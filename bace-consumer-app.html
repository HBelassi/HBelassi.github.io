Context.

The Consumer App was in its early development stages, with no prior usability tests conducted. To ensure the app delivered a seamless user experience, an exploratory usability test was initiated.

Goals: Assess the app's ease of use and identify usability barriers. Understand user expectations for guest accounts, onboarding, and problem resolution. Gather actionable insights to inform design iterations.

Key research questions: What are the primary pain points users face during the onboarding process? How do users perceive the app's overall usability, flow, and functionality? What barriers prevent users from successfully completing core tasks? What features or improvements do users expect?

Methodology.

Moderated Remote Usability Tests with 5 participants (aged 25–45), frequent online shoppers who may or may not have used a parcel locker. Tested scenarios: picking up a parcel as a guest user; creating an account and accessing additional features; resolving an issue where a locker refused to open.

Patterns.

Users found no added value in creating accounts. Many questioned the necessity and were hesitant to download a dedicated app solely for core functions. "I don't see why I need to create an account or download another app just to pick up a parcel."

Frustration with the "ping-pong" magic link log-in process. The login flow via magic link was described as tedious. Users' inboxes were flooded with multiple login emails. "Every time I log in, my email gets spammed with these magic links. It's annoying, and sometimes they expire before I can use them."

Missing vital hub instructions buried under the email CTA. Key information about accessing lockers was overlooked because it appeared after the prominent CTA button. "I didn't even see the instructions—I felt tempted to click the button."

Struggling with an unfamiliar UI. Users found the interface difficult to navigate due to bace's own branding replacing familiar retailer logos. "I didn't recognize the brands at first because their logos were replaced with the app's style. It made me feel lost."

Chatbot support expectation. Users expected immediate support for resolving issues. Sending emails was seen as too slow, and the app's default of opening the mail app caused additional frustration.

Desire for live tracking and notifications. There was strong demand for real-time parcel delivery updates. "I want to know exactly when my parcel is arriving, not just an estimate."

Key insights breakdown.

Instructions Email Ineffectiveness: Critical hub information was overlooked due to its placement after a tempting CTA button. Users suggested an intermediate step with maps or visual cues for better guidance.

Log-in Frustrations: Users disliked the redundant ping-pong log-in process. Guest accounts were more preferable unless account creation provided distinct value, like tracking history.

UI Confusion and Expectations: Users found the UI confusing as it prominently displayed bace's branding instead of the retailer's. Users anticipated live tracking similar to DHL and Amazon.

User behavior.

Post-test interviews revealed that participants frequently shop online for clothing, electronics, and groceries. While parcel lockers were valued for convenience, many preferred home delivery. Common delivery challenges included missed deliveries, limited delivery windows, package theft concerns, and return inconvenience.

Next steps.

Streamlined Log-in: Alternative log-in methods such as biometrics and social log-ins; saved session functionality to reduce repetitive log-ins.

Enhanced Onboarding: Updated email flows to prioritize essential information like hub locations with map integration; redesigned onboarding screens for clarity.

Familiarizing UI and live tracking: Iterate on the current UI; planned integration of live tracking features inspired by industry leaders.

Optimized Problem-Handling: Implemented chatbot functionality for immediate support.

The exploratory test provided critical insights that reshaped the app's roadmap and spurred cross-team collaboration with UI/UX, product management, and engineering teams.
