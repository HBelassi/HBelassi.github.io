Text Enlargement

UX Case Study — Problem Solving / UXR

Personal Project
Project Lead, UXR, UXD
Design Sprint — 2 weeks

What started as a personal experience and a challenge I encountered with one of Apple's Assistive Technologies (AT) — specifically, the text enlargement customization — turned into a full UX case study.

Add your project image here

Challenge.

Identifying the problem and conducting research to understand whether this was a common issue with other users, and to find a viable solution.

The User Journey That Led to the Problem.

On a normal day I decided to increase the security of my parents' emails and decided to change their passwords. The goal: a simple 5-minute task. Reality: 1 week of frustration.

A simple task that should have been smoothly done in 5 minutes with a positive outcome turned into a frustrating, lengthy experience — all because of the text enlargement setting on their devices.

Screenshot illustrating the auto-hyphenation issue

Auto-hyphenation.

Auto-hyphenation is a text formatting feature that automatically inserts hyphens to break long words across lines. By enlarging the system's font size, auto-hyphenation is automatically activated, resulting in words being broken mid-syllable within password fields and input boxes — which in this case proved to be a poor and unwise solution in adapting assistive technology to universal use cases.

Next steps.

Identify research goal, methodology, and target group. Formulate interview questions. Conduct 10 online interviews.

Work in progress…

This case study is actively being developed. Further research and design iterations are underway.
