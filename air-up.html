AIR_UP

Problem Solving / Industrial Design

Personal Project
Industrial Designer
Design Sprint

AirUp recognized the dullness of plain water's taste despite its vital role in hydration. The air up bottle uses scent-based flavoring technology to make drinking water more enjoyable. This project reimagines the AirUp experience by solving its key usability pain points.

Background.

This project was born from a spontaneous idea that struck me after my personal experience with the AirUp bottle. Following thorough desk research and personal experimentation, several prevalent issues were identified with the existing design.

Explaining the Magic.

Retronasal perception is the scientific principle underpinning scent-based taste. Natural flavors absorbed by your nose while drinking travel through the retronasal pathway to create the sensation of taste — without any actual flavoring in the water. While many believe taste is solely determined by tongue and taste buds, the reality is far more complex.

Ideation.

Diving into form exploration while simultaneously addressing primary concerns around hygiene, pod activation, one-hand operation, and leaking.

Hygiene.

Currently, the process involves pulling the pod up/down to activate/deactivate it, then placing the mouthpiece in contact with the pod's surface — creating hygiene concerns. A swift solution: rearrange the sequence of parts so the mouthpiece never contacts the pod directly.

Activation/Deactivation.

Simplifying the drinking process by reducing multiple steps (unscrewing cap, pulling pod, drinking, deactivating) into one single motion. The concept integrates dual functionality into the cap itself, taking reference from familiar flip-cap bottles.

One-Hand Operation.

Another objective was to make the entire process a one-handed operation. By removing the separate lid, direct drinking access is offered, loose components are eliminated, and effortless one-hand use is enabled.

Leaking.

Leakage often occurs due to bottles with short necks that only meet the cap at the edge. Expanding the contact surface area between bottle and cap ensures a complete, tight seal preventing leaks even when horizontal.

AirUp Transformation.

The reimagined design enables one-hand use with integrated pod control upon bottle opening and closing. The bottle includes a sliding lock cap with a grip, connected to a leak-proof underlay. A flexible silicon spout expands and retracts with the cap's sliding motion. The pod has four wide openings for aroma diffusion and connects to the cap for automatic activation/deactivation. Multiple functionality with one single movement — enhancing the user experience and eliminating most existing pain points.
