STAY FIT

UXR / UX

Wellness Startup
UXR, UX Designer
3 weeks

STAYFIT — an innovative app designed to cater specifically to busy individuals, offering a tailored approach to fitness and nutrition. With STAYFIT, bid farewell to the cycle of fast food and embrace a credible diet program that prioritizes your well-being.

Background.

A wellness startup is creating a new app that combines fitness and nutrition. They understand how important it is to maintain a healthy lifestyle while managing a busy schedule — and how hard it actually is. The challenge: create an app that caters to the needs of busy individuals and helps them maintain a healthy lifestyle.

Research Process.

We started the process by conducting Focus Groups rather than one-on-one interviews to encourage dynamic group discussion and diverse perspectives. Target audiences included working professionals, parents, students, and busy individuals.

Research questions included: What are the biggest challenges you face in maintaining a healthy lifestyle while juggling a busy schedule? How do you currently manage your fitness and nutrition routines? What motivates or demotivates you when it comes to prioritizing health? What specific features would you find most helpful in a fitness and nutrition app? How important is customization and personalization to you?

Focus group session documentation

Key Findings & Insights.

Time Constraints Impact Consistency: Many participants cited time constraints as a significant barrier to maintaining a healthy lifestyle.

Convenience is Key: Participants frequently mentioned the allure of convenience when it comes to food choices. Fast food and unhealthy snacks are often the default.

Motivation Fluctuates with Progress and Stress: Motivation to prioritize health is influenced by seeing progress, feeling energetic, and managing stress levels.

Need for Accessibility and Customization: There is a strong desire for fitness and nutrition solutions that are accessible, flexible, and tailored to individual lifestyles.

Accountability and Support Matter: Participants expressed a need for accountability and support in their health and wellness journey.

Ideation.

New features proposed: scheduled notifications and reminders, familiar UI, setting up routines and goals, encouraging achievement messages, device synchronization, community features, nearby healthy restaurants, healthy food recipes, various filtering options, recipes based on available ingredients, automated scheduled meal delivery, and tailored workouts based on variables.

Based on research findings, three main objectives were prioritized: the UI had to be familiar to reduce onboarding friction; avoid forcing new behaviors all at once; and tailor routines based on individual circumstances (e.g., offer office-appropriate workouts).

Next steps — Usability Testing.

Introduced innovative features that need validation to ensure they're optimized for real users:

1. Recipes based on available ingredients — allows users to browse recipes based on 3–5 available ingredients in their kitchen.

2. Automated scheduled meal delivery — the user can schedule a favourite meal from a specific restaurant to be automatically ordered and delivered.

3. Familiar UI — by placing the "LOG" button and icon exactly as the most-used apps, this increases chances of users logging their data regularly.

What to test and validate: new feature likeability, importance, and ease of use; users' feelings towards automated food ordering; UI familiarity; unnecessary features; ease of navigation; overall feelings towards the UI/UX; whether users feel the app is tailored to them; frequency of use and recommendation likelihood.
