Allcook

UXR / Industrial Design

Allcook Industries
Project Lead, UXR, Moderator, Industrial Designer

Introducing Allcook, a groundbreaking food processor meticulously crafted to revolutionize your culinary experience. Allcook is the ultimate mini cooking assistant — very compact yet powerful.

Background.

To maintain leadership in the Small Home Appliances (SHA) sector, Allcook Industries set off to conduct comprehensive user and market research. The goal: analyze user needs and challenges, keep a vigilant eye on competition and global trends, and design a product that delivers a superior experience.

Research Process.

Why? This research identifies the challenges faced by users while preparing their meals every day, and how SHA products aid or hinder that experience.

How? Recorded in-home visit interviews with observation of how users prepare their meals throughout the day. 21 interviews conducted.

Our participants mostly fell under 4 main categories: newly-wed housewives, newly-wed working wives/moms, mature family housewives, and mature family working moms.

Analysis and Synthesis.

After conducting 21 interviews, it was clear that the most typical family member to prepare or cook meals is the mother/wife. Appliances owned included choppers, blenders, hand blenders, food processors, mixers, meat mincers, and stand mixers. Leading brands owned: Moulinex, Mienta, Panasonic, Philips, Bosch, Braun, Kenwood, Fresh. Most usage occurred between 12:00–15:00 and 18:00–21:00.

Pain points.

We gathered recurring pain points during observation sessions, then grouped them under relevant categories by noticing patterns and similarities. Hidden pain points were also identified — users were adapting around design flaws, thinking it was their fault or "just how the design works." That's a key benefit of live observation sessions: a designer can identify weaknesses invisible to users themselves.

Competitors audit.

Auditing benchmark models and brands specified by the marketing and brand manager's team, comparing attributes including aesthetics, functionality, ergonomics, materials, pricing, and market positioning.

Ideation Process.

Brainstorm: Gathered the entire ID team for a quick brainstorm session, including people not involved in the research phase to ensure fresh perspectives.

Down selection: Project leads gathered and discussed all proposed ideas, choosing the most promising directions to move forward with.

Create: After down-selecting to 3 promising directions, further development of keywords and ideas into more defined concepts.

Develop and Validate.

After discussion with the design manager, the 3 directions were adjusted and further developed in 3D CAD, then 3D printed to conduct swift usability tests with physical mock-ups.

Refine.

Having physical mock-ups at scale 1:1 allows a designer to truly feel every aspect of the form and design — proportions, ergonomics, and interactions that can't be experienced on screen.

Finalize.

After several iterations, testing, and stakeholder validation, one winning direction was finalized. Allcook is the ultimate mini cooking assistant — very compact yet powerful. You would regret all the past years you spent without it.
