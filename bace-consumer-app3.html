To finalize the app's flow for the beta launch, I collaborated closely with the design and development teams to test direct interactions with the locker. The primary focus was evaluating user preferences for two proposed log-in methods: social login vs. OTP SMS. Another key task was investigating the "delivery status" feature as a practical compromise for the previously requested live tracking feature. Learn more about Phase I & II →

Recap.

After two rounds of research, key discoveries shaped the Consumer App's development: a web app was the most user-aligned and cost-effective choice for beta launch; users faced unclear locker instructions, a confusing magic link log-in, and an unfamiliar UI; the lack of live tracking and guest log-in frustrated users; users preferred chatbot support over email-based assistance.

Context.

Goals: Evaluate which log-in/sign-in method is more successful and preferred. Validate usability of the web app, ensuring pain points from earlier phases were resolved. Investigate the "delivery status" feature as an alternative to live tracking. Confirm the app is ready for beta launch.

Key research questions: Which log-in method (social login vs. OTP SMS) is preferred? Which has higher success rates? Are users able to complete core tasks efficiently? Does the "delivery status" feature meet user needs? How satisfied are users with overall usability?

Methodology.

A/B Testing (50 participants): Group A (25): Social login. Group B (25): OTP SMS login. Metrics tracked: success rates, time to log in, error rates, customer satisfaction.

Live Usability Testing + Post-Test Survey (10 participants): Un-moderated sessions with actual bace lockers. Tasks included logging in, navigating to a locker location, retrieving an order or returning an item, and reporting a malfunction. 5 participants per A/B group.

Live usability test at bace locker — real-world interaction

Key insights.

OTP SMS emerged as the preferred log-in method. Task success rates reached 90%.

30 seconds — average login time with OTP SMS vs. 60 seconds with social login.

4.8/5 — user satisfaction score for OTP SMS, citing simplicity and reliability.

OTP SMS stood out for its simplicity, speed, and low error rate. Social logins were seen as inconvenient, particularly for users without accounts on suggested platforms. "I loved how simple the OTP SMS option was—it was quick and didn't require me to remember another password."

Overall Usability: 92% of participants rated the app as easy to use, up from 70% in Phase I. Minor issues like inconsistent button labels were noted for refinement.

Delivery Status: Users found delivery status updates efficient and aligned with familiar services like DHL and Amazon. "I don't really need live tracking—just knowing when my package is shipped and when it will arrive is enough."

Reflection.

Conducting live tests with users interacting directly with actual bace lockers provided critical, real-world insights unavailable in lab settings. The three-phase research journey demonstrated the value of iteration: from uncovering usability gaps in Phase I to validating a launch-ready product in Phase III.

Challenges included coordinating live testing logistics (ensuring locker availability, scheduling participants) and addressing feedback from live testing while adhering to tight pre-launch timelines.

Next steps.

Launch beta with OTP SMS log-in. Fine-tune user flow and polish the app's overall design. Work closely with the marketing team on market entry strategy. Establish a performance monitoring strategy with dashboards and feedback loops for continuous optimization.
