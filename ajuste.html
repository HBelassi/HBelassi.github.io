AJUSTE

UXR / Industrial Design

Personal Project
Project Lead, UXR, Senior Industrial Designer
3 weeks

AJUSTE is a Spout Vacuum bottle designed to improve the user experience and tackle common problems in the vacuum bottle market. The name reflects its unique selling point — free adjustment to the user's needs.

Background.

A need emerged to design a new Vacuum bottle targeted towards the US market, that can compete with leading brands. I conducted 45 online interviews via Zoom from US metropolitan states including California, New York, Texas, and Florida.

Market Research.

Desk research to identify leading bottle brands in the US market and understand their value propositions. Research goals: identify leading brands, document features and technologies, understand brand persona, point out value propositions, assess value vs. cost, review customer feedback, track trending CMF, learn from points of weakness, understand material and manufacturing limitations.

Add your project image here

Analysis and Synthesis.

Participants were analyzed as owners vs. non-owners. Types of bottles: coffee tumbler, wide mouth, spout, soft straw, hard straw. Most frequently owned brands: YETI, TAKEYA, CONTIGO, STANLEY, HYDRO FLASK, THERMOS. Preferred materials: stainless steel (majority), followed by plastic and stainless+ceramic. Most used on weekdays. Top factors affecting buying decisions: usability/functionality, design/aesthetics, cost.

Pain Points.

Bottle slipping, mobility limitations, nose contact, lid hitting nose/eye when drinking, stability on surfaces, surface scratching, metal friction sound, cleaning difficulty, beverage residue near spout, outdoor refill challenges, not fitting in cup holders, metal taste, stains from dark beverages, forgetting to drink, difficulty reaching the last amount.

Business Goals.

304 Stainless steel body, plastic lid, double vacuum layered bottle, Chinese manufacturing, selling price $45–55, initial batch 25,000 pcs, sustainable packaging, 4–6 color variations, customization upon request.

Brainstorming.

An affinity map helped distill insights from the research. Ideas were classified into Primary-Form Shapers (factors that affect the form drastically) and Secondary-Details (add-ons adaptable to any given form).

Ideation and Testing.

Rapid sketching followed by CAD modeling and 3D printing to conduct swift usability tests with physical mock-ups, evaluating promising ideas against criteria like intuitive use, ergonomics, and manufacturing feasibility.

Development and Refinement.

One design proved very promising based on insights from initial mock-up testing. After several iterations, AJUSTE was finalized — named to reflect its unique selling point of free adjustment to the user's drinking style and needs.
